02 — The Campaign Pipeline

From First Hello to GX1

Every interaction is a step. The goal is not to sell — it's to educate, elevate, and activate. Here is the full flow from initial conversation to GX completion.

01
Touch Point — Discovery

The Warm Conversation

Whether in the OR break room, at a DHS provider meeting, a church lobby, or after a financial literacy class with teens — this is where curiosity is sparked, not a pitch delivered.

  • Ask about their current financial situation — goals, concerns, what keeps them up at night
  • "What does retirement look like for you?" / "Do you have a plan or a product?"
  • "If you couldn't work tomorrow, how long would your family be okay?"
  • Listen 80%, talk 20%. Qualify interest. Book next step.
02
Education — Value First

Financial Literacy Workshop or 1-on-1

Deliver value before asking for anything. This is our brand: we teach first. This is our credibility in the community and what separates us from every other financial rep they've met.

  • Offer a free 60-min workshop (virtual/Zoom or in-person/office) — no obligation
  • Topics: cash flow, debt elimination, protection gaps, wealth-building basics
  • For youth/teens: money habits, compound interest, credit, first job income
  • For OR professionals: debt payoff, income protection, tax-advantaged accounts
  • For foster youth: budgeting, banking basics, understanding insurance
03
Assessment — Learn Their World

The Financial Needs Analysis (FNA)

After the workshop, invite them to a complimentary financial review. This is where we go deep — and where we discover exactly what they need so we can prescribe the right solution.

  • Schedule via Zoom or office — 45–60 min dedicated session
  • Cover: income, expenses, protection, savings, debt, goals, timeline
  • Use WFG's FNA tool — present the "money triad" (earn, protect, grow)
  • Identify gaps: no life insurance, no IUL, no retirement plan, wrong beneficiaries
  • Do NOT present solutions yet — build the picture first
04
Presentation — What We Do

The WFG Solution Presentation

Now we show them what WFG does, what we provide, and how it maps to exactly what they told us. Not a generic pitch — a mirror of their needs.

  • WFG overview: independent, client-first, multi-carrier — not a captive agent
  • Show the product suite: Term · IUL · Annuities · LTC · Debt Elimination · College Planning
  • Present the "Financial House" concept — foundation = protection, growth = roof
  • Share relevant client story (similar profession/situation — anonymized)
  • Introduce the business opportunity — briefly, authentically: "Have you ever thought about what you'd do if you could add income while helping people?"
05
Decision — Client Path

Application or Follow-Up

After the presentation, close with clarity. Some say yes now, most need one follow-up. Either path is win — we are building trust and a referral engine either way.

  • If ready: run the app together in the meeting (life insurance, IUL, etc.)
  • If not ready: schedule a 2nd meeting — bring a senior teammate or upline for credibility
  • Always ask for 3 referrals: "Who else in your circle is like you — busy, successful, unplanned?"
  • For OR professionals: leverage colleague referrals — one surgeon knows 20 more
  • Log in CRM. Set 30/60/90 day follow-up calendar
06
Opportunity — Business Path

The WFG Business Conversation

For those who show entrepreneurial interest during the financial literacy sessions, provider meetings, or FNA — introduce the WFG opportunity as a natural extension of the education mission.

  • "We actually train people just like you to do what I do — help families while building income"
  • Share your story and your upline's story — real numbers, real timelines
  • Invite to a Business Opportunity Meeting (BOM) — virtual or in-office
  • Target: DHS providers, nurses, faith leaders, teachers — they already trust people
  • Foster youth alumni and community youth: plant the seed early, license when 18+
07
Activation — GX Builder

Recruiting to GX1 Promotion

Every client is a potential recruit. Every recruit is a path to GX. Track your builders, support their first clients, and celebrate their first promotions. GX1 is a team achievement — not just yours.

  • Set clear GX promotion goals with each new associate: recruits + premium targets
  • Run weekly accountability calls — virtual or in-office
  • Use the 3-way call structure to help new associates close their warm market
  • Bring new recruits to compassion circle / provider meetings as observers
  • Celebrate every milestone publicly — social media, team meetings, community shoutouts
03 — Delivery Formats

How We Show Up

We meet people where they are — in their breakroom, on their lunch Zoom, in church fellowship halls, and in our office. Every format is designed to educate and activate.

Virtual — Zoom

Zoom Workshops & 1-on-1s

Best for busy professionals (surgeons, anesthesiologists), out-of-area contacts, and anyone who won't drive in. Low barrier, high reach.

  • 60-min group Financial Literacy Workshops — slides + Q&A
  • 45-min private FNA appointments — Zoom + screen share
  • Weekly financial education series (recurring Zoom link)
  • Foster youth and teen sessions — recorded for replay
  • Pastor/provider pre-meeting: 15-min intro presentation
In-Person — Office

Office 1-on-1 & Small Group

For serious prospects ready to sit down. Higher conversion rate. Professional environment builds trust and credibility, especially for larger policy conversations.

  • Private FNA and solution presentation — 60–90 min
  • Couples / family financial review sessions
  • BOM — Business Opportunity Meeting for new recruits
  • Licensed associate training sessions
  • 3-way sits with senior SMD for high-value clients
Community — Events

Community Workshops & Partnerships

Our strongest differentiator. We go to them — community centers, churches, schools, provider meetings. This is where the WFG mission lives in LA County.

  • Provider/compassion circle educational sessions — Long Beach
  • Church financial stewardship workshop series (Sunday or midweek)

04 — Conversation Scripts

What to Say, Word for Word

Authenticity wins — but having a framework keeps you sharp. Use these as a guide, not a script. The goal is to listen, connect, and book the next step.

OR Professional — Break Room / Hallway

"Hey, quick question — I know we're both slammed but I've been doing financial workshops for physicians in the area. A lot of surgeons I talk to are making great money but haven't had time to set up a real plan. Not selling anything — I literally just do free financial reviews. Would you ever want to grab 45 minutes? I'll come to you, or we can do it on Zoom on your schedule."

DHS Provider / Pastor — Compassion Circle Meeting

"I love what you're doing for these families. One thing we've been helping with is financial literacy — everything from teaching kids about money to helping families get protected and start building. We do free workshops, totally no-pressure. Would it make sense for me to do a short presentation at one of your meetings or through your network? The community could really benefit."

Foster Youth / Teen — After Financial Literacy Class

"You asked a really smart question today about investing. I do this full-time — helping people understand money and build a plan. Your generation has a huge advantage if you start now. Would you be down to do a deeper dive — either with your guardian or just for yourself? I'll bring the pizza if we do it in person." [Smiles and energy matter here most.]

Elite Athlete / Player Family — Event or Introduction

"Congratulations on everything — that's amazing. I work with a lot of young people in your situation and honestly, the ones who win long-term are the ones who built a financial plan early — not just a signing bonus. No rush, no pressure, but if you'd ever want to sit down and just map out a picture of where you are and where you want to be, I do that for free. Who's handling your financial plan right now?"
