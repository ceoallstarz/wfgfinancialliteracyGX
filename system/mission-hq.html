Trustworthy Selling · EPR Framework

The 5 Levels Deep Why

Surface answers don't build lasting motivation. Go five levels deep with every associate and prospect to find the real reason they're here — the one they'll fight for when things get hard.

L1
Surface Level
"Why did you join RFM?"
Common: "To make extra money." — Not enough to sustain action when things get hard.
L2
Going Deeper
"Why do you need the extra money?"
Typical: "To pay off debt / help my family." — Still general. Keep going.
L3
Getting Specific
"What does helping your family look like — specifically?"
Getting real: "My mom is struggling. I want to buy her a house."
L4
Emotional Core
"What would that mean to you?"
The feeling: "She sacrificed everything for me. She's never had financial security."
L5
The Real Why — Write This on Your Mirror
"So when things get hard — what are you really fighting for?"
"I'm fighting to change the story for my family — forever."

Field-Ready · Social Events & Strangers

Handling "What Do You Do?"

You will get this question everywhere. Have one clean answer ready — then stop talking and let them reveal themselves.

Short Version — Social Event, Stranger
"I have an agency where I get people licensed and trained to do complimentary financial plans for businesses and families — saving on taxes, building wealth, anything to do with money."
Then ask: "What do you do?" — and stop. Let them talk. The silence after your answer is intentional. Don't fill it.
When they ask "Like what?"
"Most people are paying more in taxes than they need to, or they have life insurance that's working against them. We look at the full picture and fix whatever's broken."
When they seem interested
"Honestly it's easier to show you than explain it. I do a free 20-minute financial review — what does your schedule look like next week?"
